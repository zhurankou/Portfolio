Intro

Goal – design a prototype of a Pomodoro-type mobile application with a To-Do functionality.

"The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method... The technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks."

Pomodoro Technique, Wikipedia

Approach

  1. Analyzing existing Pomodoro applications, identifying the target audience, common patterns, and design flaws.
  2. Designing, prototyping, and testing user flows and interactions.
  3. Using the feedback to iterate on design decisions and create final deliverables.

Takeaways

  • Target Audience – people with difficulties of focusing on completing tasks.
  • Users seek for the ability to organize tasks into lists, flexibile timers and better editing, removing, and skipping tasks functionality.
  • Finally, the users appreciate the simplicity and clear hierarchy of the content on the screen, including tasks' titles and descriptions.

Therefore, improving these features creates new design opportunities.

Process

During the brainstorming session I came up with three general directions for tasks organization and interactions,

  1. Card stacks layout for current tasks "A-La-Tinder".
  2. Card lists organized by categories in a panel view.
  3. App integrated with iMessage where tasks start only if they are shared with contacts.

In my class peer review I received positive feedback for the Idea #3. It was also my favorite one, because this interaction motivates people to acomplish tasks they planned and shared, applying the factor of acountability.

Such interaction could potentually become a core feature of the product, playing a vital role in attaracting new customers.

User Flow

Step 1. My initial idea was to eliminate unnessesary interaction in the App and use the native messaging platform to create tasks by sharing them with friends and relatives.

Step 2. Then, the user would receive a notification and use application timer with Start|Pause|Stop functionality.

Step 3. Upon complition of the task he or she would have an option to send a confirmation to the person who they shared the task with.

Initially, to maintain the acountability theme, I decided to name the application as Promise and the tasks as promises.

Iterations

Version 1. In my first prototype I used default iOs UI elements and light pink colors as a refference to "pomodoro" and "pinky promise" themes.

Version 2. In the second iteration I experimented with a different, more "squared" look. I also added the ability to add and substract 5 minutes intervals in the timer.

The changes in both iterations were based off of the feedback I received during peer reviews and testing exercises, such as "speak-out-loud".

Pivot

After reviewing user testing results and feedback from my peers, I identified a few drawbacks in the existing design,

  • The transition between different applications seemed confusing. Testers would like the flow to be more intuitive.
  • The dependancy on sheduling task only via sending a message felt a little bit too pursuasive. Also, the experience of receiving notifications on the "friend-side" might have been disturbing.
  • The overall visual aestetics could have been improved.

Based on these findings, I decided to rethink the application's flow from the ground up, instead of iterating on my design.

I reverted back to reviews from the users of existing applications. Most of them would just like to see a simplified flow of creating tasks, list organizations, and more intuitive timer interactions. Besides that, some users wished to integrate better options for reusing accomplished tasks.

A was also tend to revise the mandatory sharing option of creating task to be more user-friendly.

Design Sessions

During participatory design sessions with pomodoro apps' users, we came up with three main userflows.

Based on the Hick's Law, "the more choices a person is presented with, the longer the person will take to reach a decision."

Session 3 results in this case looked very promissing. It had the least amount of screens and navigation choices and at the same time provided all the nessesary options.

User Flow

Main Screen. Upon openning the app, user would add new tasks to make a To-Do list, then select them and choose between Delete | Start Timer options.

Right Screen would have the list with active Timer and the To-Do list. It also would allow to change time for Work and Rest intervals.

Left Screen would be giving an option to scroll through the past tasks in Feed and Calendar views and repeat them by adding to the To-Do list in the Main Screen.

Details

Colors

I chose to use a combination of pure Black (#000000), White (#FFFFFF), and medium-dark Grey (#999999) colors to provide better contrast for the accessibility. The primary Blue has a light Purple tone, which associates with stability of Blue and the energy of Red.

Typography

My typography choice was the Circular Std which is a modern, friendly Sans Serif typeface with simple geometrical forms. It provides a veriety of weights and great readability on screens.

Icons

I desidned a set of icons using simple geometrical shapes with rounded corners which matches the curves of the Circular Std typeface. The Play | Pause | Next icons contain conventional meaning for better usability.

Spacing

To mantain the elements' consistancy across the screen, I used the 8-pt Spacing Template for Sketch. I developped this symbol library, using Spacing method described in Google Material Design, to speed up my design workflows.
